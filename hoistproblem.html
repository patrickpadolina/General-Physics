Problem Statement Context & Specifications
An electric hoist lifts a steel beam vertically at a constant speed. The hoist raises the beam through a height of $d = 18.0\text{ m}$ in $t = 12.0\text{ s}$.

The mass of the steel beam is $m = 1500\text{ kg}$. During the lift, the guide rails exert a constant frictional force of $f = 250\text{ N}$ opposing the motion.

Assume acceleration due to gravity $g = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$.
Height Raised ($d$) $18.0\text{ m}$
Time Duration ($t$) $12.0\text{ s}$
Beam Mass ($m$) $1500\text{ kg}$
Frictional Force ($f$) $250\text{ N}$
Interactive Motion Visualizer Constant Velocity Motion
1. Vertical Motion: The hoist applies upward tension on the cable to lift the $1500\text{ kg}$ steel beam.
2. Guide Rail Friction: As the beam moves up, contact with side guide rails produces a downward resistive friction force $f = 250\text{ N}$.
3. Zero Acceleration: Because velocity is constant ($a = 0\text{ m/s}^2$), net vertical force must equal zero according to Newton's 1st Law ($\Sigma F_y = 0$).
Building the Free Body Diagram (FBD) Step-by-Step Force Vectors
Step 1: Point Mass Core
Represent the $1500\text{ kg}$ steel beam as a central point mass object.
Step 2: Downward Gravity (Weight $W$)
Gravity pulls downward: $W = m \cdot g = (1500\text{ kg})(9.81\text{ m/s}^2) = 14,715\text{ N}$.
Step 3: Downward Friction ($f$)
Guide rails resist upward motion with downward force: $f = 250\text{ N}$.
Step 4: Upward Motor Force ($F_{\text{lift}}$)
To maintain constant velocity, motor tension balances both downward forces:
$F_{\text{lift}} = W + f = 14,715\text{ N} + 250\text{ N} = 14,965\text{ N}$.
GRESA Solution Strategy Given & Required
G — Given
  • Beam mass: $m = 1500\text{ kg}$
  • Height lifted: $d = 18.0\text{ m}$
  • Time duration: $t = 12.0\text{ s}$
  • Frictional force: $f = 250\text{ N}$ (downward, opposing motion)
  • Gravitational acceleration: $g = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$
  • State of motion: Constant velocity ($a = 0\text{ m/s}^2$)
R — Required
  1. Weight of the steel beam ($W$)
  2. Lifting velocity ($v$)
  3. Total force motor must overcome ($F_{\text{lift}}$)
  4. Power required by the motor ($P$)
GRESA Solution Strategy Equations
E — Equations
1. Weight Formula:
$$W = m \cdot g$$
2. Constant Velocity Formula:
$$v = \frac{d}{t}$$
3. Force Balance Equation ($\Sigma F_y = 0$):
$$\Sigma F_y = F_{\text{lift}} - W - f = 0 \implies F_{\text{lift}} = W + f$$
4. Power Formula:
$$P = F_{\text{lift}} \cdot v$$
GRESA Solution Strategy Step-by-Step Variable Substitutions
S — Solution
1. Weight of the Steel Beam ($W$)
Formula: $W = m \cdot g$ Substitute: $W = (1500\text{ kg}) \cdot (9.81\text{ m/s}^2)$ Result: $W = 14,715\text{ N}$
2. Lifting Velocity ($v$)
Formula: $v = \frac{d}{t}$ Substitute: $v = \frac{18.0\text{ m}}{12.0\text{ s}}$ Result: $v = 1.50\text{ m/s}$
3. Total Motor Lifting Force ($F_{\text{lift}}$)
Formula: $F_{\text{lift}} = W + f$ Substitute: $F_{\text{lift}} = 14,715\text{ N} + 250\text{ N}$ Result: $F_{\text{lift}} = 14,965\text{ N}$
4. Required Motor Power Output ($P$)
Formula: $P = F_{\text{lift}} \cdot v$ Substitute: $P = (14,965\text{ N}) \cdot (1.50\text{ m/s})$ Result: $P = 22,447.5\text{ W}$

A — Answer
